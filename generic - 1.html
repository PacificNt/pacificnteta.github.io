Microsfot Excel

As mentionned ealier, the project started by importing pdf bank statemts into Excel. This was done by querying the needed data from the pdf bank statement into Excel. A copy of one of the bank statements to see how the bank statements looked and have an idea of which data were excluded and which ones were queried into Excel can be accessed here.

After querying the needed data to Excel, I then did an initial cleaning by transforming the data before loading it using Power Query. With Power Query, I removed empty columns created when querying the data from pdf to Excel, I merged some columns, changed some data types and replace values such as 'null' by '0'. After transforming and loading the data, I did some more cleaning by replacing the year '2024' in the 'date' column by '2023' as in the initial tables from the pdf files, there was no year so Power Query assumed that it was '2024'. I repeated these steps for all the 8 bank statements from June 2023 to January 2024; I only kept the year '2024' for the January bank statement. In the last step, I combined all the tables/bank statements into one named 'June_to_January' by appending the tables.

The pre-cleaned 'June_to_January' table as well as the individual tables for each month can be accessed here.

    

SQL Server

After pre-cleaning the data in Excel, I imported the data in SQL Server to further clean it before working with it. I started by checking if the table was perfectly imported and checked errors on which to resume the data cleaning. And after that I aslo checked the data types of all the columns to see where conversion will be applicable.

The following points/errors were noticed :

  • The presence of letters ('Cr') in the columns 'Amount' and 'Balance'. These two columns are supposed to contain numbers only, the presence of 'Cr' will not make calculations on these columns possible.
  • The presence of commas (',') in instead of full stops('.') to delimit decimals in the 'Amount' column. This will also make calculations in this column. These two issues will be addressed later.
  • The columns 'Amount' and 'Balance' are NVARCHAR which is a strong data type but it should be a number data type. And the 'Date' column apprears as 'datetime' but since the time part doesn't reflect any info, we can keep it just as 'date'. These will be fixed later.

After noticing the problems mentionned above, I then proceeded to address them. I started by removing the commas ',' as decimal delimiters and replaced them with full stops '.'. After this I proceeded to change data type of the 'Date' column from "datetime" to "date". After this, I cleaned the 'Description' column by removing '[image]' that was present in some rows.

ACTIONS TO TAKE

  • 1.Multiply or diversify source of income.
  • As seen during the analysis, the account holder relies heavily on 'ATM Cash Deposit'(accounting around 83% of income). This also increases the banking fees with amount being deposited every month. Finding another source of income will help the account holder not only to not rely heavily on 'ATM Cash Deposit' but also reduce banking fees on ATM Cash deposit.
    • 2.Budget.
  • Budget Expenses
    • Many economist suggest to have 50/30/20 or 50/40/10 budget plan refering to 50% of income going to the savings, 30% or 40% going to the needs and 20% or 10% going to the wants. In the case of the account holder, around 81% of his spending goes to 'Purchases and Payments' which are not all needs. 81% way beyond the needs. The accout holder should make a list of his needs and wants to allocate only 30 to 40% to his needs and 10 to 20% of its to his wants. The rest should go to the savings
    • The expense budget should go down as far as to the subcategories. Based on the behavioral and the average spendngs, the account holder should allocate a specific budget(amount) to subcategories such as 'Airtime' or 'Groceries & Toileries'. The spending on these two subcategories are pretty close; the spending on 'Airtime' should be reduced and budgeted.
    • Budget savings
  • As said above, around 50 % of income should go to the savings but unfortunately for the account holder, only 7% goes to the savings. The account holder should review his spendings and reduce it so that 50% of the income should go to the savings.
  • 3.Manage Balance Fluctuations.
    •
  • There is a huge difference between the highest and the lowest balance and this inconsistency is seen throughout the whole period. By applying the measures mentionned above and being financially disciplined, the account holder can keep the balance consistent.
